It's easy to fall into the habbit of working with the same or similar tech stacks, even when switching jobs. You're comfortable in your tools and you feel that you can be productive faster. After all, many job postings do ask for skills in particular programming languages, so it's a natural part of the system.

The problem is that you then also get limited to what products you can work with! New languages pop up and it's a good idea to at least dip your feet and have a feel for how it is to do some simple program.
